Featured Attractions

Hanging Coffins of Sagada

The Hanging Coffins of Sagada are an example of a long-standing burial custom that the native Igorot people have followed for more than 2,000 years. According to this tradition, coffins are positioned inside caves or draped on cliff faces to bring the departed closer to the sky. Deep reverence for ancestors and a conviction in making the transition to the afterlife easier are reflected in this tradition.

Located at: Echo Valley, Sagada, Mountain Province, Philippines

Colors of Stobosa

Also known as StoBoSa Hillside Homes Artwork, the sitios of Stonehill, Botiwtiw, and Sadjap are represented by the portmanteau Stobosa. The project covered an area of more than 18,000 square meters and entailed painting between 150 and 200 homes. The design turns the formerly boring hillside into a lively tourist destination with its expansive patterns of sunflowers and rainbows.

Located at: ELa Trinidad, Benguet, Philippines

Sumaguing Cave

The Sumaguing cave showcases multiple unique rock forms that have been sculpted over years ago. Spelunking enthusiasts frequently visit it because of its remarkable limestone formations, which include stalactites, stalagmites, and natural ponds.

Located at: Sagada, Mountain Province, Philippines

Mt. Pigingan

The Ibaloi word "Piging," which means "slanted," is the source of the name "Pigingan," which reflects the mountain's characteristic inclined profile. Mt. Pigingan provides hikers with a demanding and rewarding experience despite its comparatively small elevation. The trail offers expansive views of the nearby mountain ranges and river valleys due to its diverse terrain, which includes broad meadows, ridges, and steep ascents.

Located at: Barangay Dalupirip, Itogon, Benguet, Philippines.

Blue Soil Hills

The remarkable bluish-green tint of the Blue Soil Hills, a natural phenomenon surrounded by tall pine trees, provides visitors with a tranquil and surreal experience. The hills' complex ridges and folds, which resemble brain gyri, are reflected in the name "Kaman-utek," which means "like a brain" in the local dialect.

Located at: Balili, Bontoc, Sagada, Mountain Province, Philippines

About the Cordillera Region

The Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR), which includes the main mountain ranges of the Cordillera Central, is the only landlocked area in the Philippines. It is frequently referred to as the "Watershed Cradle of North Luzon" since it is the origin of several important rivers.

The CAR offers a distinctive vacation experience because of its cold climate, pine-covered mountains, and indigenous culture.

Quick Facts

  • Provinces: Abra, Apayao, Benguet, Ifugao, Kalinga, Mt. Province.
  • Climate: Temperate and cool year-round.
